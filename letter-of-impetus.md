# Letter of Impetus

As I continue to dive into the world of agentic AI, acknowledging the new normal for our industry, learning the (current) limitations of these tools, I am realizing that solid documentation will be a primary predictor of product success.

This is intuitive. Documents that exist can drive agents and their context. Documents that do not exist cannot. As such, it behooves us as users of these tools to have documentation that is thorough, concise, structured, and usable by both human and agent team members.  

I have a .NET background. I am used to spending the majority of my time in the high level language with only the occasional need to peer into lower levels of abstraction. With agentic development, I view the documentation as the high level of abstraction, and only occasionally will I need to look at the low level code. I'm sure this is not an original idea. The paradigm shift that I am in the midst of is that I will no longer primarily spend my time reading code, I will spend it reading documents.

I do not claim to be a documentation expert. My role has traditionally fallen within the realm of developer, and I've only had to consume/produce types of documents related to that role. To maximize the value that these new agentic tools can provide, I must become familiar with the documents that exist across the team at all roles. I must understand both their content and their lifecycle. I must also understand the limitations of these tools so that the documentation can work with instead of fight against them.

The Docstitution is my attempt at learning what works and what doesn't when it comes to making documents useful for a software team. Which documents should be included? How should they be organized? Is the strategy intuitive enough that everyone on the team can take their individual information, know where to put it, and know how to format it to turn it into team knowledge? Can agents use them effectively?

The Docstitution is intended for teams producing software products. How other industries and disciplines manage their documents is outside the scope of this effort. It is a living document; I will update it as I continue to learn and the industry matures around best practices. My hope is that even if you do not use it directly, it can help you think about how to organize your projects.

Semper Reformanda.
